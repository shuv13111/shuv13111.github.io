The Problem

Supply-chain analysts fielded roughly 100 ad-hoc questions a week — "which SKUs are exposed if this supplier slips?", "what's the alternate routing for this lane?" Each answer meant manually joining data across systems: an average of 6 hours per question, often longer.

The knowledge existed. It was just scattered across millions of entities and relationships no single query could reach.

The Approach

Model the supply chain as a knowledge graph, then let an agent traverse it. I designed a Neo4j graph of 18M nodes and 54M edges — suppliers, SKUs, lanes, facilities, purchase orders — and built a LangGraph agent on Gemini that translates natural-language questions into graph traversals, validates intermediate results, and composes a sourced answer.

  • Agentic query planning: the agent decomposes questions into Cypher traversals and self-checks results
  • Retrieval grounded in the graph — every claim in an answer traces to specific nodes
  • Latency budget enforced per step; 38s average end-to-end

Guardrails

Production AI needs the unglamorous parts done right. Every response passes bias scans and prompt-toxicity filters before reaching a user; the pipeline has held 100% policy compliance since launch. The same RAG foundation was extended to automate compliance review — cutting a 4-hour manual process to 10 minutes.
